Mendelian Genetics

Mendel established the basic principles of inheritance using pea plants. He identified patterns that describe how traits are passed down.

  • True breeding — offspring identical to parents
  • Hybrids — offspring differ from parents
  • Dominant alleles — expressed when present
  • Recessive alleles — masked if dominant is present
  • Homozygous — identical alleles (GG or gg)
  • Heterozygous — different alleles (Gg)
Mendel’s Laws of Inheritance

Law of Segregation — alleles separate into different gametes.

Law of Independent Assortment — alleles of different traits separate independently.

Monohybrid Cross

A cross between two heterozygous individuals for one trait (Yy × Yy).

P1: one homozygous dominant × one homozygous recessive.

F1 — all heterozygous.

F2 — phenotypic ratio 3:1; genotypic ratio 1:2:1.

Dihybrid Cross

A cross between individuals heterozygous for two traits.

F2 offspring show:

  • Phenotypic ratio: 9:3:3:1
  • Genotypic ratio: 1:2:2:1:4:1:2:2:1
Test Cross

Used to determine an unknown genotype by crossing it with a homozygous recessive individual.

  • All dominant offspring → genotype is homozygous
  • 50% dominant, 50% recessive → genotype is heterozygous
Back Cross

A cross between an F-generation offspring and one of its parents.

If the parent is homozygous recessive, it can also serve as a test cross.

Reciprocal Cross

Same cross but parents’ genotypes are switched.

Example:

  • Monohybrid: RR × rr
  • Reciprocal: rr × RR
Central Dogma of Molecular Biology

Information flows from DNA → RNA → Protein.
