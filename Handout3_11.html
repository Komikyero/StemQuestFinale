Summary of Plasma Membrane and Transport Mechanisms

The plasma membrane acts as the boundary between a cell's internal and external environments and regulates all substances entering and exiting the cell, exhibiting selective permeability.

Membrane Structures

Proteins are crucial molecules responsible for most internal and external interactions of the cell.

Click this link for more info
Protein category: Function: Examples:
Transmembrane Proteins Regulate the movement of molecules across the membrane, forming the cell's transport mechanisms Carriers, Channels, Receptor proteins
Interior protein network Determines and maintains the structure and form of the plasma membrane, responsible for cell shape and attachment of macromolecules. Spectrins and Clathrins.
Cell-surface markers Responsible for the recognition of foreign and local (self) cells and tissues. Glycoproteins and Glycolipids

Six Classes of Membrane Proteins

Transporters - Responsible for the movement of molecules/solutes across the membrane.

Enzymes - Required for chemical reactions for metabolic functions.

Cell-surface receptors - Detect chemical messages from the environment to elicit reactions.

Cell-surface identity markers - Proteins that identify the cell's origin and type.

Cell-to-cell adhesion proteins - Form bonds and junctions between cells.

Attachment to the Cytoskeleton - Anchor proteins to maintain cell shape and stability.

What is Tonicity?

It is the difference in osmotic concentration that influences water movement.

Hypertonic - Higher solute concentration: water leaves animal cell (shrivels/crenates).

Hypotonic - Low solute concentration: water rushes into animal cell (swells/bursts/lyses).

Isotonic - No difference in osmotic concentration: animal cell is normal.

Summary of Atoms, Molecules, and Ions

Chemistry began in ancient times through metal extraction but became a true science in the 18th century. Modern understanding of matter is built on Dalton’s Atomic Theory, later refined with the discovery of subatomic particles and isotopes.

Dalton’s Atomic Theory

Dalton proposed that matter is made of indivisible atoms, identical within each element, and combined in fixed ratios. Modern science modified this by discovering subatomic particles and isotopes.

Click this link for more info
Postulate: Description:
Indivisible atoms All matter is made of tiny particles called atoms.
Identical atoms Atoms of the same element are identical (revised due to isotopes).
Conservation Atoms cannot be created or destroyed in chemical reactions.
Definite composition Compounds form from atoms in fixed whole-number ratios.

Fundamental Laws of Matter

Law of Conservation of Mass – Mass is neither created nor destroyed.

Law of Constant Composition – A compound always has the same proportion of elements by mass.

Atomic Structure

Protons – positively charged; found in nucleus.

Neutrons – neutral; found in nucleus.

Electrons – negatively charged; found outside the nucleus.

Atomic Number (Z) – number of protons; identifies the element.

Mass Number (A) – total number of protons and neutrons.

Molecules and Ions

Molecules – atoms bonded together (e.g., H₂, O₂).

Covalent bond – sharing of electrons.

Ionic bond – transfer of electrons forming ions.

Cations – positively charged (lose electrons).

Anions – negatively charged (gain electrons).

Chemical Formulas

Molecular formulas – show exact atoms (H₂O, CO₂, C₆H₁₂O₆).

Structural formulas – show atom arrangement and bonds.

Naming Ionic Compounds

Monatomic ions
• Cation – element name (e.g., Sodium).
• Anion – root + “ide” (Chloride, Oxide).

Polyatomic ions
Use memorized ion names (Sulfate, Nitrate, Phosphate).

Transition metals – have multiple charges; use Roman numerals (Iron(II), Iron(III)).
