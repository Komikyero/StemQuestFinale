What is ATP?

ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) is the cell’s energy currency. It has three phosphate groups, ribose sugar, and an adenine base. Its phosphate bonds store high energy due to repelling charges.

ATP–ADP Cycle

Phosphorylation releases energy (exergonic).
Dephosphorylation requires energy (endergonic).

The ATP–ADP cycle continually regenerates ATP using energy from cellular processes.

Metabolic Pathways

Metabolic pathways are sequences of chemical reactions in cells. They maintain cell structure and function and require enzymes to accelerate reactions.

Cellular Respiration
  • Glycolysis – Cytoplasm
  • Krebs Cycle – Mitochondrial matrix
  • Electron Transport Chain – Inner mitochondrial membrane
Stage 1: Glycolysis

Net products:

  • 2 ATP
  • 2 NADH
  • 2 Pyruvate
Stage 2: Krebs Cycle

The cycle produces CO₂, ATP, NADH, and FADH₂. Molecules are recycled, which is why it’s called a cycle.

Stage 3: ETC & Chemiosmosis

NADH and FADH₂ donate electrons into the chain for gradual energy release. The final electron acceptor is oxygen.

Anaerobic Respiration

Occurs without oxygen. Produces lactic acid (muscle cramps) or alcohol (used in fermentation).

Photosynthesis
  • Light-dependent reactions Granum
  • Light-independent reactions (Calvin Cycle) Stroma

What is Stoichiometry?

Stoichiometry is the study of the quantitative relationships between reactants and products in chemical reactions.

It allows us to predict how much of a substance will be used or produced in a chemical reaction.

  • Used to calculate product yields.
  • Used to determine required reactant amounts.
  • Based on balanced chemical equations.

Stoichiometry connects mass, moles, and particles using the mole concept and balanced equations.

Chemical Composition Concepts
  1. Atomic Mass: Weighted average mass of all isotopes.
  2. Mole: \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) particles.
  3. Percent Composition: % by mass of each element in a compound.

What is the Mole?

  • 1 mole = \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\) particles.
  • The mass of 1 mole (grams) equals the atomic/molecular mass.
